The Origins of Hip-Hop

The Bronx

In the 1960s and 1970s, the Bronx faced severe socio-economic challenges, including poverty, gang violence, and urban decay. Amidst this, African American and Latino youth turned to music, dance, and art as forms of expression, giving rise to block parties. These gatherings became the breeding ground for hip-hop, a cultural movement that would change the world.

The Creation

Hip-hop began in the 1960s with funk and soul breakbeats from artists like James Brown, leading to DJ Kool Herc’s 1973 party at 1520 Sedgwick Avenue, where he introduced breakbeats. This then brought popularity to four things DJing, MCing, break dancing, and graffiti in the Bronx which led to the Sugarhill Gang’s massive 1979 hit "Rapper’s Delight," which brought rap to the rest of the world.
